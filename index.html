Gettysburg National Military Park, 1195 Baltimore Pike, Gettysburg, PA 17325 False Napoleon - 6 pounder made to look like 12 pounder - Muzzle face of 6-pdr Field Gun Model 1841 along Hancock Avenue at Gettysburg. This gun was produced by Revere Copper of Boston, Mass. in 1861. It is registry number 1, of two, produced by the firm for Federal orders. It weighed 853 pounds when inspected by Joseph P. Farley. The gun was altered to a "false Napoleon" by the Gettysburg Battlefield Board in the 1890s to make up for a shortage of 12-pdr Napoleons in the battlefield displays. Much of the muzzle markings were destroyed during the modifications. Muzzle Pictures\Our Pictures\False1.jpg
